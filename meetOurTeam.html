Meet Our Team

Director, Mary Niang

Mary Niang obtained her bachelor's degree in Business Administration and Accounting from Saint Joseph's University. Mary received her MBA and Project Management certificate from Georgetown University. Mary also received her MBA from Bellarmine University. She speaks four languages. She served in the United States Army as a specialist with the 3/1 infantry division. She owned and ran “Classic Supply Chain”, specialized in appearance enhancement and beauty services based in Staten Island, New York. Mary is a loving mother of 4 boys. Upon her military discharge in 2014, Mary worked for the Federal Government until 2020 she decided to finally pursue her dreams of working with kids.

Assistant Director, Norma Nangju

Norma Nangju received a B.S.in Environmental Engineering from Swarthmore College. She also received a M.S. in Environmental Engineering, a M.A.T in Elementary Education, and an Ed.S. in Educational Administration from University of Louisville. She is currently pursuing an Ed.D. in Educational Leadership and Organizational Development from University of Louisville. She has 20 years of teaching experience.
Todder/PreK Teacher, Mohammadi Begum

Mohammadi Begum received her Bachelors of Commerce from Osmania University and an MBA in Finance and Human Resources in Hyderabad, India. She also completed a postgraduate Diploma in Early Childhood Education from the International Teacher Training Program in Bharati Vidyalaya College of Education and Training. After working as a US taxation consultant, she is excited to transition into her new profession educating and inspiring young children.

Infant Teacher, Marwa Munaf

Marwa Munaf previously worked at Baghdad Daycare in Baghdad, Iraq and at Nur Early Childhood Center in Louisville, KY. Currently, she is pursuing a degree in Nursing at Jefferson Community & Technical College. She enjoys playing with children, exploring music and nurturing and caring for infants and toddlers.

School Age Teacher, Rona Osman

Rona Osman grew up in Louisville, KY and has previously worked at Nur Islamic School of Louisville as a Language Arts and Math tutor. Over the past year she has worked as a private tutor to elementary and high school students. Aside from working with kids, Rona is a photographer. She loves capturing portraits of people and animals, landscapes, and journalistic photography. Rona uses her penchant for art to expand her students' world view-for them to see that their lives are surrounded by poetry, art, and stories. While at the University of Louisville, Rona was a speech mentor-helping students tell their stories and sharpen their public speaking skills. She was also historian for the Muslim Student Association.

School Age Teacher, Tabarek Kadhim

Tabarek Kadhim is a student at the University of Louisville majoring in Molecular Biology. Last year, she worked full time in Jordan to teach conversational English to college and school age students at Hammurabi Cultural Center. She participated in summer internships teaching Elementary students through Teach Portland. She is publishing an English conversational book in Jordan. Tabarek is not only an educator, but a global citizen. Currently, she is running an online educational program to help underprivileged college students with their conversational English. She plans to continue her Masters in Education.
