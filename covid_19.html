Review our COVID-19 Guidelines


♦ We will abide by maximum group size specified by the state. Children will remain in the same group all day without being combined with another classroom. The same staff members will work with the same children each day in order to reduce additional exposure, including the staff members that give breaks to primary staff members.

♦ Entrance to YLA will be limited to: staff, first responders, Department for Community Based Services, Division of Regulated Child Care, necessary utility workers, professionals providing medical/therapeutic services for children with special needs, children enrolled in the facility, parents or legal guardians of children enrolled in the program.

♦ YLA will use video/virtual observations for virtual tours for prospective families. Virtual tours will be done after hours.

♦ For pick up and drop off, please call the main number at YLA. A staff member will come out to assist in taking the children into or out of the center to eliminate unnecessary traffic to classrooms and exposure of children. Parents and custodial adults are requested to wear cloth face coverings or masks during pick-up and drop-off. Parents and custodial adults will log in online when they drop off or pick up their child.

♦ YLA staff members will conduct group activities that can be performed while observing social distancing (e.g. coloring/painting) and limit sharing of toys/items. Outdoor instruction and outdoor fitness classes will be provided for children of appropriate age. YLA shall stagger playground time between classroom groups. YLA will not hold center-wide family events or field trips.

♦ YLA will not have “lost and found” bins.

♦YLA will not use of water fountains. Children are encouraged to bring their own water bottles.

♦ YLA is requesting registration and payments to be done online to the greatest extent practicable. For those that cannot use contactless payments, please put payments or registration forms in an envelope and drop in the mailbox. If needing to enter the building, parents or legal guardians should adhere to markings of distances of six (6) feet minimum in cashier queuing areas.

♦ Meetings between parents and teachers will also be conducted through phone or online.

♦ YLA will ensure the cleaning and sanitation of frequently touched surfaces with appropriate disinfectants, in accordance with appropriate Kentucky child care standards. Frequently touched surfaces that are regularly disinfected include door knobs and handles. Cleaning will not include procedures that could re-aerosolize infectious particles. This includes avoiding practices such as dry sweeping or use of high-pressure streams of air, water, or cleaning chemicals. The cleaning and sanitizing plan will be posted.

♦ Toys that cannot be cleaned and sanitized such as machine washable cloth toys will not be used.

♦ Toys that children have placed in their mouths or that are otherwise contaminated by bodily secretions will be set aside in a separate container marked for soiled toys until they are cleaned by hand by a person wearing gloves.

♦ Children’s books, like other paper-based materials, are not considered a high risk for transmission and do not need additional cleaning or disinfection procedures. Plastic infant and toddler books will be cleaned and sanitized as the material allows.

♦ Parents or legal guardians are asked to bring a bin to store each child’s bedding (sheets, pillows, blankets, sleeping bags) separately. The bin containing bedding will be sent home weekly to be cleaned.

♦ Parents or legal guardians are asked to bring a bin to store each child’s toys. The toys will be washed and sanitized daily by the teachers. Toys will not be shared between children.

♦ Parents or legal guardians are asked to bring the breakfast, lunch, and snacks for each child. Food will not be shared between students.

♦ YLA will schedule restroom breaks and wipe the restroom after every use.

♦ YLA will ensure that staff members and children practice hand hygiene frequently. Staff members and children will wash their hands upon arrival for the day, after breaks, upon returning from outside, after toileting or assisting a child with toileting, after each diaper change or pull-up change, after contact with bodily fluids or cleaning up spills or objects contaminated with bodily fluids, after cleaning or sanitizing or using any chemical products, before eating, serving or preparing food or bottles or feeding a child, before and after completing a medical procedure or administering medication, when visibly soiled (must use soap and water), and prior to departure. Staff will assist children with hand hygiene.

♦ YLA will encourage staff members/employees and children to limit touching their mouth, nose, and face.

♦ YLA will provide hand sanitizers, tissues, and waste baskets in every classroom.

♦ YLA establish a process that follows CDC guidelines to address when any individual is identified, suspected, or confirmed as a COVID-19 case.

♦ All YLA staff are tested for covid before beginning work.

♦ Children and adults will be screened for fever and contagious symptoms upon entry into YLA.

♦ Staff members who demonstrate symptoms of COVID-19 will be tested for the illness before returning to work. Children or adults that test positive for COVID-19 must follow the recommendations of their local health department on when to return to the childcare program.

♦ When a child shows a fever or other contagious symptoms, the child will be removed from the classroom immediately and placed in a safe, secluded area. The parent or guardian must remove the child from the childcare program within one hour.

♦ YLA will notify enrolled families and staff of a diagnosed case of COVID19 in the program, while still protecting the privacy of the diagnosed individual.

♦ YLA has the required cleaning supplies and PPE (masks, latex/non-latex gloves) on site.

♦ All staff members receive training on cleaning and sanitizing procedures

♦ YLA will train staff how to use PPE. This training includes: when to use PPE; what PPE is necessary; and how to properly put on, use, and remove PPE.

♦ YLA will maintain information for potential contact notification

♦ YLA ensure employees are informed that they may identify and communicate potential improvements and/or concerns in order to reduce potential risk of exposure at the workplace. All education and training will be communicated in the language best understood by the individual receiving the education and training.

♦ Adults must wear a face mask while inside YLA unless doing so would represent a serious risk to their health or safety or they are more than 6 feet away from any other individual.

♦ Children who are five (5) years of age or under will not wear masks due to increased risks of suffocation and strangulation. YLA recommends to the parents of children over five (5) that their child wear a mask and provide information about the benefits of masking.

♦ Childcare providers will wear gloves while serving food and preparing bottles. Gloves will be changed between bottle feedings.

♦ YLA will make masks available for parents and custodial adults where in-facility interaction is necessary. Parents and custodial adults may bring and use their own face mask. YLA may refuse those individuals entrance to the facility who do not wear a face mask. Parents or custodial adults who are unable to wear a face mask may call the office and staff may assist parents online.

♦ Childcare programs will ensure employees use gloves when cleaning equipment, toys, playspaces, workspaces, and hight-ouch areas of the facility.

♦ YLA will ensure gloves are available to employees engaging in high-touch activity to the greatest extent practicable provided that they do not create additional hazards while being worn.

♦ YLA ensures employees wear gloves while handling products during drop-off and pick-up as well as during any shipping and receiving.
